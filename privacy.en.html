2. Philosophy: what we DO NOT collect

Reclaim is designed under the principle of data minimization: we collect the bare minimum needed for the service to function. The following information never leaves your device and is never transmitted to us:

  • The list of apps installed on your phone. Reclaim reads it locally only to let you choose which apps to block.
  • Your geographic location. Geofencing functions operate entirely on the device.
  • The content of notifications from other apps. Reclaim may suppress notifications from blocked apps but never reads or transmits their content.
  • Screenshots, screen recordings, accessibility text-to-speech buffers.
  • Browsing history, web searches, data from other apps.

2.bis Device permissions: how Reclaim uses Accessibility, Location, Foreground Service and Notifications

Reclaim requires four sensitive Android permissions to provide the app's main feature, namely blocking the distracting applications you have chosen. This section transparently describes what they do and — most importantly — what they do not do.

2.bis.1 Accessibility service (Android — BIND_ACCESSIBILITY_SERVICE)

Purpose: the accessibility service is the technical mechanism by which Reclaim detects when you open an app you have previously chosen to block. Upon detection, it shows an overlay screen that prevents you from using the distracting app and returns you to the phone home screen.

What it concretely does:

  • Receives from the operating system "active window change" events (TYPE_WINDOW_STATE_CHANGED)
  • Compares the package name of the foreground app with your list of blocked apps (stored locally)
  • If the app is in the list, triggers the block (overlay + return to home)

What it does NOT do (explicit commitment by Solnovium SRLS):

  • ❌ Does not read screen content of other apps (text, photos, messages)
  • ❌ Does not record user activity inside apps
  • ❌ Does not send to our servers nor to third parties the names of opened apps, usage times, or the list of installed apps
  • ❌ Does not profile the user for marketing or advertising purposes
  • ❌ Does not use the accessibility service for any purpose other than the declared app blocking

Data handling: all information processed by the accessibility service (package names, opening timestamps) stays exclusively on your device and is never transmitted externally. The list of blocked apps is saved in the app's private SharedPreferences.

Permission grant: the permission is explicitly requested during onboarding ("Block permissions" screen). You must enable it manually from Android Settings → Accessibility → Reclaim. You can revoke it at any time from the same settings.

Legal basis: contract performance (art. 6.1.b GDPR) — the accessibility service is strictly necessary to provide the app blocking feature you signed up for.

2.bis.2 Background location (ACCESS_BACKGROUND_LOCATION)

Purpose: background location is used exclusively for the optional geofencing feature: automatically blocking distracting apps when you enter areas you have configured (for example workplace, school, gym).

What it concretely does:

  • When you configure a geofence from the app (Modes → Location), Reclaim registers the coordinates you choose with the Android operating system via the Google Play Services Geofence API
  • The operating system autonomously monitors your location and notifies Reclaim only when you enter or exit the configured areas
  • On an ENTER event, Reclaim activates the block on the associated apps; on EXIT, it unblocks them

What it does NOT do (explicit commitment by Solnovium SRLS):

  • ❌ Does not continuously track your location — receives only point-in-time ENTER/EXIT events at the zones you configured
  • ❌ Does not send coordinates to our servers or to third parties
  • ❌ Does not share location with advertising or marketing platforms
  • ❌ Does not build a map of the user's movements
  • ❌ Does not use location for any purpose other than the declared geofencing

Data handling: the coordinates of the geofences you configure are saved exclusively on your device in private SharedPreferences. They are never synchronized, transmitted, or stored on our servers. Upon app uninstallation they are automatically deleted by the operating system.

Optional permission: this feature is fully optional. If you do not configure any geofence, Reclaim never requests the location permission nor accesses location data. You can use all other app blocking features without granting this permission.

Permission grant: the permission is requested only at the moment the user chooses to activate the geofence feature during onboarding ("Location-based blocking" screen) or later from Modes → Location. On Android 11 and later, "while using the app" location is requested first, and separately the "all the time" (background) extension is requested via the standard system flow.

Legal basis: contract performance (art. 6.1.b GDPR), limited to users who voluntarily activate the geofencing feature.

2.bis.3 Foreground service (FOREGROUND_SERVICE_SPECIAL_USE, subtype screen_time_monitoring)

Purpose: to allow the block overlay to appear immediately even when the Reclaim app is not in the foreground, Reclaim runs a foreground service called BlockerService. The service is declared in the Android manifest with the screen_time_monitoring subtype, the specific Android category for screen time monitoring (digital wellbeing) functionality.

Service behavior:

  • Starts only when the user has at least one active block (Timer, Schedule, Location, or Launch Limit).
  • Stops automatically when the user deactivates or ends all blocks.
  • Is event-driven: reacts to accessibility service events, does not perform continuous polling.
  • Shows a persistent and user-visible notification in the status bar ("Reclaim attivo — Protezione in esecuzione"), as required by Android rules, so that you are always aware that the service is active.

Data handling: the service does not collect, store, or transmit any data. It only locally manages the block overlay and the state of any active geofences. No information leaves the device.

Legal basis: contract performance (art. 6.1.b GDPR) — the service is strictly necessary to ensure the responsiveness of the app blocking feature.

2.bis.4 Notifications (POST_NOTIFICATIONS)

Purpose: starting from Android 13 (API 33), the runtime POST_NOTIFICATIONS permission is required to display the persistent foreground service notification described in section 2.bis.3. Reclaim requests this permission at the moment the user activates the first block.

Exclusive use:

  • ✅ Display the persistent foreground service notification (mandatory under Android foreground service rules).
  • ❌ We do not send promotional notifications, marketing pushes, suggestions, reminders, or other types of notifications.
  • ❌ We do not transmit data or events associated with notifications to external servers.

If you deny the permission, the app continues to work but the foreground service notification will not be visible in the status bar; the block remains active as long as there is a configured block.

Legal basis: contract performance (art. 6.1.b GDPR).

3.3.bis Marketing & advertising campaign measurement

To measure the effectiveness of our advertising campaigns (e.g. ads on Meta — Facebook/Instagram) and optimize marketing spending, we collect the following events and transmit them to Meta Platforms Inc.:

  • App Install, App Open, session duration (collected automatically on first launch and every app open)
  • Sign Up, Start Trial (recorded after voluntary paywall confirmation), Subscribe (recorded after actual charge, when we activate payments)

Together with these events the following are sent to Meta:

  • Device advertising identifier: only Android Advertising ID (on iOS we do NOT send the IDFA — see paragraph below)
  • Device model, operating system, language, country (technical aggregated information)
  • No email, no username, no personal Reclaim data (block statistics, app list, location)

On iOS, Reclaim does not show any tracking prompt. Campaign attribution is performed exclusively via Apple's privacy-preserving SKAdNetwork system, which provides Meta with aggregated statistics (e.g. "X installs from campaign Y") without identifying the individual user. Your iPhone's IDFA is never read nor transmitted by Reclaim to Meta. On Android attribution happens via Advertising ID (manageable from Android Settings → Privacy → Ads → "Reset advertising ID" or "Disable ad personalization").

Provider: Meta Platforms Inc. (United States). See section 6 for details on extra-EU transfers and DPA. Meta's full Privacy Policy describes data handling on their side.

Retention period on Meta's side: conversion events are retained by Meta for approximately 90 days for attribution and campaign optimization purposes; statistical aggregates up to 1 year. For data access or deletion requests on Meta's side you can use the official tool: accountscenter.facebook.com.

You can disable this collection on Reclaim's side at any time from the same "Anonymous analytics" toggle in Profile. The preference applies to both PostHog (product analytics) and Meta (marketing): a single opt-out turns off both.

3.4 Fully anonymous aggregated data

Reclaim maintains a single aggregated counter called total_distractions_avoided, summing the total number of times the entire user base has resisted opening a blocked app.

This counter:

  • is a single integer (e.g. 12,847,392) — it contains no user_id, no per-user timestamp, no geolocation, no time bucketing or any other identifier;
  • is mathematically impossible to use to trace a single user's activity, even when combined with other information;
  • under GDPR Recital 26 is classified as anonymous information and does not constitute personal data;
  • is the only data that may be publicly displayed (on our website as a "live counter", in marketing materials) to communicate the cumulative impact of the Reclaim community.

No other data — not even aggregated — is shared, sold or transferred to third parties. In particular we do not share: aggregated demographics, geographic heatmaps, hourly statistics, public rankings, leaderboards or any other form of aggregation that could carry re-identification risk.

4. Sign in with Google

Reclaim offers login via your Google account ("Sign in with Google"). When you use it:

  • The flow is native on the device (account sheet on iOS, Credential Manager on Android), without a web browser.
  • We receive from Google your email address and a Google unique identifier (sub field).
  • Due to technical limits of the Android Credential Manager API (Google Identity Services), Google also transmits, without us being able to opt out upfront: first name, last name, profile picture, locale, email verification flag.
  • Privacy minimization (`_scrubProviderMetadata`): within milliseconds of login, Reclaim automatically invokes on our database (Supabase) an operation that deletes the first name, last name, profile picture, locale and other accessory attributes received from Google. We retain only email + unique identifier. This procedure is verifiable in source code (file auth_service.dart, method _scrubProviderMetadata).
Privacy minimization is applied automatically and is not disable. It is visible in server logs as an updateUser operation with null values on profile fields, executed immediately after authentication.

5. Sign in with Apple

Reclaim offers login via your Apple ID ("Sign in with Apple"). When you use it:

  • We receive from Apple an email address and an Apple unique identifier. The email may be your real one (if you chose "Share my email") or an alias from Apple's "Hide My Email" service, in the format xxxxx@privaterelay.appleid.com.
  • Apple does not transmit by default first name, last name, profile picture, locale: our code does not request such fields (scope reduced to email only).
  • Any accessory fields Apple might transmit in the future are nevertheless discarded immediately via the same Privacy Minimization procedure described in section 4.

5.1 Hide My Email — Apple's email anonymization

If you choose "Hide My Email" at first sign-in with Apple, Apple generates a unique random alias for Reclaim (e.g. mxk9p3vqj7@privaterelay.appleid.com). For us that alias is your email in every respect: we do not know and will never be able to know your real email address.

When we send you transactional emails (e.g. account deletion confirmation), Apple receives them at the alias and forwards them to your real address through its own mail relay. The email content passes through Apple servers acting as neutral intermediaries.

Important: at any time you can disable Apple's email forwarding from your Apple ID settings (iOS Settings → Apple ID → "Sign in with Apple" → Reclaim). If you do:

  • Our emails to that alias no longer reach you.
  • Reclaim has no way to know that the relay is disabled.
  • Service notifications (e.g. deletion confirmations) will be silently dropped.

To restore the communication channel you can: (a) re-enable the Apple relay, or (b) log in to Reclaim and manually update your real email in Profile → Settings.

7. Extra-EU data transfers

Data managed by Supabase and PostHog stays entirely within the European Union (servers in Ireland and Germany respectively).

Data managed by Apple, Google, Meta, RevenueCat and Resend may be transferred to the United States or other extra-EU countries. Such transfers are covered by Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) approved by the European Commission and — where applicable — by certification under the EU-U.S. Data Privacy Framework (Apple, Google, Meta are DPF-certified). You can request a copy of the standard contractual clauses by writing to the privacy email.

8. Retention period

Active account: your data is retained while your account is active.

Account deletion: when you delete your account from the app's Profile:

  • Your data (account, statistics, diary) is deleted immediately from the main database table.
  • Daily safety backups still containing your data are rotated and definitively deleted within 30 days.
  • After 30 days from deletion, no residual copy of your data exists in our systems.

We do not maintain long-term backups (monthly or yearly) for personally identifiable data.

9. User rights (arts. 15–22 GDPR)

You can exercise the following rights at any time:

9.1 Right of access (art. 15)

You can request a copy of your personal data by writing to privacy.reclaim@solnovium.com. We provide the export in JSON format within 30 days of the request.

9.2 Right to rectification (art. 16)

You can update username, email and other profile data directly in the app (Profile → Settings). For changes not available in the app, contact us.

9.3 Right to erasure (art. 17, "right to be forgotten")

You can delete your account at any time from Profile → "Delete account". The operation deletes all your data (see section 8). Alternatively you can request deletion via email at the privacy address.

9.4 Right to restriction (art. 18)

You can request that we restrict the processing of specific data (e.g. retain but not use it for analytics) by writing to the privacy address.

9.5 Right to data portability (art. 20)

You can export your data directly from the app, instantly: Profile → "Your data" → "Export your data (JSON)". The data is copied to your device clipboard in structured JSON format (`profile`, `journeys`, `daily_stats`) and you can paste it anywhere (notes, email, cloud drive) for archival or transfer to another service.

Alternatively you can request the export via email at the privacy address: in that case we provide the JSON file within 30 days of the request.

9.6 Right to object (art. 21)

You can object to the processing of your data for direct marketing purposes (which we do not perform) or for analytics. For analytics: use the "Anonymous analytics" toggle in Profile. For other processing: contact us.

9.7 Right not to be subject to automated decisions (art. 22)

Reclaim does not use automated profiling or algorithmic decision-making processes that produce legal effects on the user.

10. Cookies and trackers

The Reclaim app does not use traditional cookies (it is a native app, not a website).

PostHog analytics uses a random identifier stored device-side in SharedPreferences (Android) or UserDefaults (iOS). This identifier is not a cookie, is not shared with third-party advertisers, and can be cleared by disabling analytics from Profile (a new ID will be generated if you re-enable).

Reclaim does not contain: advertisements, advertising trackers, marketing SDKs, device fingerprinting, cross-app retargeting.

11. Security

We adopt technical and organizational measures appropriate under art. 32 GDPR:

  • Data transmission exclusively via HTTPS/TLS 1.3
  • At-rest encryption of the Supabase database
  • User passwords hashed with bcrypt
  • Session tokens (JWT) with limited expiration
  • Row-Level Security (RLS) on the database: each user accesses only their own data
  • Access to the Supabase database restricted to authorized Solnovium SRLS personnel only
  • Audit logging of administrative operations
